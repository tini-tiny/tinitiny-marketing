## 3. Digital Channels Overview

### 3.1 Instagram (@tini.tiny.shop)
**Status:** 14,200+ organic followers. Primary revenue-driving social channel.
**Approach:** Editorial lifestyle content. Overhead product shots. Unboxing. Community content. Stories for closeness, feed for aesthetics.
**Content pillars:**
- Product beauty (overhead mat shots, styling, room reveals)
- Behind the scenes (packaging, production feel — not technical detail)
- Community (cool moms club, lifestyle)
- Social proof (customer reposts, reviews, re-orders)

**Paid:** R$800–1,550/month on Instagram + Facebook. Facebook session growth is explosive (+4,800% from paid campaigns). Target audience: premium CEPs in SP/RJ.

### 3.2 TikTok (@tini.tiny.shop)
**Status:** Active but underdeveloped — highest growth priority.
**Approach:** Short-form video that is slow-paced, cinematic, aspirational. Never fast-cut. Always beautiful. No music embedded — added natively on TikTok.
**Content format:** 1080×1920, Poppins Bold text overlays, normalized brand colors, zero frontal child faces.
**Scheduling:** Buffer for pre-scheduling. Music added after upload in TikTok app.

**Content pillars for TikTok:**
1. **Product beauty** — satisfying overhead shots, slow zoom, minimal text
2. **Process / technology** — how UV printing works (no machine details), safety certification
3. **Unboxing** — the packaging experience as content
4. **Comparison** — generic mass-produced mat vs Tini Tiny (visual contrast)
5. **Mom identity** — "motherhood is not boring" messaging, Cool Moms Club
6. **POV / lifestyle** — barefoot babies, wooden toys, airy interiors

**What NOT to do on TikTok:**
- Fast edits / flash cuts
- Frontal child faces
- Embedded music (add natively)
- Generic stock-looking content
- Oversaturated or filtered photos

### 3.3 Pinterest
**Status:** To be activated. High-intent audience for home décor + premium baby.
**Priority boards:**
1. Tapetes & Quartos — mat styled in beautiful nurseries and living rooms
2. Quartos Inspiração — premium baby/kid room inspo (not just Tini Tiny products)
3. Cool Moms Club — lifestyle content for the target mother
4. Unboxing & Presentinhos — packaging, gift-giving, baby shower

**Setup checklist:**
- Verify site domain in Pinterest Business → Settings → Claim
- Install Pinterest Tag on Shopify (free, native app)
- Enable product catalog sync with Shopify
- Publish 10 pins before advertising

### 3.4 Google
**Status:** Very low organic traffic (4 users/month from Google Organic). Major untapped opportunity.
**Paid (planned):** CEP-targeted campaigns for high-income neighborhoods in SP/RJ.
**Target CEPs:** Jardins, Itaim Bibi, Vila Nova Conceição (SP) / Leblon, Ipanema, Gávea (RJ) / Batel (Curitiba) / Moinhos de Vento (Porto Alegre)

## 4. Content Strategy

### The Golden Rules for All Content
1. **Brand aesthetic above all** — if it doesn't look like Tini Tiny, it doesn't go out
2. **Product is the hero** — the mat is always the most beautiful thing in the frame
3. **No frontal child faces in video** — overhead, behind, looking down only
4. **Consistent color treatment** — bright, airy, slightly desaturated
5. **Slow pacing on video** — minimum 3.5s per frame, text stays minimum 4s
6. **Font:** Poppins Bold for all digital overlays and marketing text
7. **Brand bar:** Always end content with "tini tiny | @tini.tiny.shop" in sage

### Weekly Content Cadence (Target)

| Channel | Frequency | Format |

| TikTok | 1× daily, 7×/week | Video (MP4, 1080×1920) |

## 5. Conversion Optimization

### The Problem: 82% Checkout Abandonment
Our single biggest revenue opportunity. Of users who reach checkout, 82% do not complete. The target is 60% in 30 days.

**Actions underway / to implement:**
- [ ] Parcelamento (installments) visible **above the fold** on every product page
- [ ] PIX 8% discount visible at checkout and on PDP
- [ ] Abandoned checkout email automation (Shopify native — Marketing → Automations)
- [ ] Retargeting ads for checkout abandoners (Facebook/Instagram)
- [ ] Delivery time estimate visible on product page before checkout
- [ ] Mobile checkout tested on both iOS and Android

### Email Capture — Cool Moms Club
**Strategy:** Build email list without offering a discount. The incentive is **exclusivity**, not a coupon.
**Popup copy:** "Entre para a lista das Cool Moms. Acesso antecipado a novas estampas. Lançamentos exclusivos. Dicas para quartos incríveis."
**Tool:** Klaviyo or Privy on Shopify
**Welcome email:** Personal, warm, no hard sell. Signed "Camila, fundadora da Tini Tiny."

## 7. Influencer Strategy

**Philosophy:** Organic first. We brief influencers with product, they have creative freedom. No scripted endorsements. Authenticity is the product.

**Tier approach:**
- Macro (500K+): @lalarudge, @nicolesarfaty — for campaign moments (Dia das Mães, launches)
- Mid (50–200K): @marcellaminelli, @marianajabur — ongoing brand affinity
- Micro (5–50K): @biavergueiro, @coollectstudio — community trust

**Brief template:**
- Send product with full packaging experience (it's content in itself)
- Share brand story (brand guide)
- Single ask: post when it feels natural
- Never: ask for specific captions, script the post

**UGC (User-Generated Content):**
- Repost customer content with permission — always credit
- Screenshot and save every tag for future ad creative
- Goal: 20% of Instagram feed is real customer content

## 9. What We Do Not Do

- No discounts to acquire customers (use exclusivity and value instead)
- No loud, saturated, or generic-looking creative
- No fast-cut video editing
- No corporate or clinical language
- No paid followers or engagement pods
- No campaigns that don't align with the brand aesthetic — if it looks cheap, it doesn't go out
